My Role

As the lead designer in this project, I collaborated with PM, engineer lead, and researcher in the early stage to come up with potential solutions. And created two concepts and prototypes to test out which design works better through usability study.

Background

Groupon is known as a platform where people can find great local deals nearby. Savings and deals have always been emphasized on the product page from the beginning. Accordingly, the deal-centric model really shapes the customer's mindset and behaviors while they are using Groupon. In 2017, Groupon wanted to increase customer's purchase frequency by getting "high intent" customers. We found the deal-centric model is not the best way to achieve this goal from the user research. Therefore, we started to seek for the design solution to transform product page to be more merchant-centric.

What's the Goal

  • Create a merchant-centric product page to support high intent users‚Äô ideal experiences to find local services.
  • Enable users to find all the merchant information they need within Groupon.
  • Help users discover all the offerings from the same merchant in one location.

While also making sure that

  • Users still can easily find the deal information (e.g., fine print, about deal)
  • Users are able to quickly identify the information belong to deal or merchant
  • It‚Äôs easy for users to take action on purchase.

Define Target Users: High Intent Customers

To understand the behavior of high intent customers. From the research, we found deals or savings aren‚Äôt always the priority in most high intent use cases. High intent users need a wide range of merchant information such as location, hours etc to make a purchase decision.

Biggest pain point: too much cross-referencing

In order to figure out which treatment to get and find a reputable with a good idea, Groupon customers have to look up information at different sites or even call the merchant. It's time-consuming and laborious.

Idea Generation Stage

For big scale project, it always great to start with thinking broadly and creatively without limitation. At that time, I had a brainstorming session with main stakeholders, which included PM, lead engineer, designers, user researcher, to come up with couple ideas.

After collecting the feedback and ideas from the brainstorming, I came up with following two directions: compound version & one page version.

Usability Study

To evaluate the usability of the two proposed designs, I worked with user researcher to conduct a study. During the study, I iterated the design at the end of the day to make sure problems had been fixed before next day's testing. This strategy lets us be able to receive the immediate feedback on the tweaks we just made. .

Key Takeways from Usability Study

The main goal of comparative usability study isn‚Äôt to declare a winner per se but to identify the best and worst of the proposed experiences and improve them. However, the experience of landing participants on a merchant/offering driven page was the better experience.

  • User's expectation of the product page changes when they come from different entry points (e.g, tap merchant first v.s tap deal first)
  • Users expect to see all the merchant information is readily available in the first place
  • Display all deal options in one view instead of hidding in the dropdown
  • Bring clarity to merchant attributes
